### üó∫Ô∏è Part 1: What's Out There? (The Competitive Landscape)

My analysis of the current market shows that competitors fall into four distinct categories. **Your idea, WingThing, is the only one I've seen that bridges the gap between them.**

1.  **The "AI Soulmate" (e.g., Replika, Kindroid)**
    * **What they do:** They are designed for deep emotional, and often romantic, companionship. They excel at validation, "always-on" chat, and creating a judgment-free "friend" (as Replika's marketing states).
    * **Where they fail (for your user):** They are a *silo*. They are designed to *replace* human connection, not foster it. They can increase isolation and are often criticized for their passive, agreeable nature. They are a fantasy, not a real-life support system.

2.  **The "AI Therapist" (e.g., Wysa, Woebot)**
    * **What they do:** These are the clinical, "therapy-lite" bots you (correctly) want to avoid. They are explicitly built on evidence-based frameworks like Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT).
    * **Where they fail:** They are *not* companions. They are tools for managing specific mental health conditions like anxiety or depression. The user is a "patient," which is the exact opposite of your positioning.

3.  **The "AI Generalist" (e.g., Pi, ChatGPT)**
    * **What they do:** They are incredibly intelligent, kind, and useful conversationalists. Pi, in particular, is positioned as a "personal AI" for emotional support.
    * **Where they fail:** They are largely ephemeral. They have limited persistent memory and are *reactive*. They wait for you to talk. They won't remember you were dreading a parent-teacher conference and proactively check in on you afterward.

4.  **The "AI Utility" (e.g., Trevor AI, ParentGPT, Samsung's "Now Brief")**
    * **What they do:** These are productivity-focused. They help you plan your day, answer parenting questions, or summarize your schedule.
    * **Where they fail:** They are cold. They are task managers. They can manage your calendar but have zero emotional context. They don't know *why* that 2 PM meeting is stressing you out.

**The "WingThing" Gap:** Nobody is combining the **warm, persistent memory** of the "Soulmate" with the **proactive, real-world utility** of the "Utility" and then using it as a **bridge to real human connection.**
